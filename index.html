About

Sukriti Gupta is a Ph.D. student in Computer Science and Engineering at IIT Delhi, advised by Prof. Amit Kumar and Prof. Naveen Garg. Her research sits in theoretical computer science, where she designs and analyzes approximation algorithms for submodular optimization — problems whose diminishing-returns structure recurs throughout combinatorial optimization, and where the optimum is provably hard to reach, so the real question is how tight a guarantee one can prove.

Before returning to research, she spent more than three years building production systems where the cost of a slow algorithm is measured in microseconds. As a quantitative developer and systems engineer at AlphaGrep Securities and Tower Research Capital, she worked on low-latency C++ market-data and order-routing infrastructure, signal research, and high-throughput trading systems across global exchanges. That experience left her with a performance-obsessed, rigorously empirical instinct for algorithm design — a sense for what theory buys you in practice — that now sharpens the questions she chooses to prove things about.

She earned a dual B.Tech. + M.Tech. in Computer Science from IIT Delhi with a specialization in theoretical computer science, and has taught algorithms, theory of computation, and computational geometry to hundreds of students — earning an Outstanding Teaching Assistant award. Outside the proofs, she mentors young women and visually impaired students preparing for higher education in STEM.

Research Interests

Provable guarantees for hard problems

I study approximation algorithms — polynomial-time methods that come provably close to the optimum for problems that are intractable to solve exactly. Much of my work centers on submodular optimization, where a natural diminishing-returns structure unifies a wide range of problems across combinatorial optimization and yields a flexible, principled algorithmic toolkit.

01

Submodular Optimization

Exploiting the diminishing-returns structure shared by coverage, influence, diversity, and cost-sharing phenomena to design unified, principled algorithms.

02

Approximation & Hardness

Polynomial-time algorithms with worst-case guarantees for intractable problems — paired with matching lower bounds that reveal where the limits lie.

03

LP Relaxations & Rounding

Linear and configuration LPs, multilinear and Lovász extensions, contention resolution, and primal–dual and local-search methods as routes to tight guarantees.

04

Combinatorial & Integer Optimization

Structural and polyhedral arguments for discrete optimization problems at the interface of theory and large-scale systems.

05

Algorithms under Uncertainty

Online and competitive analysis — making decisions that stay near-optimal when the input arrives over time and the future is unknown.

Approximating Optimal Broadcast of Files in a Hose-Model Network

Thomas Erlebach, Naveen Garg, Sukriti Gupta, Amitabh Trehan. 45th IARCS Annual Conference on Foundations of Software Technology and Theoretical Computer Science (FSTTCS), 2025.

We study how to broadcast a file among nodes with arbitrary, asymmetric upload and download capacities in a hose-model network, minimizing the time (makespan) by which every node finishes receiving the file — under both migratory and non-migratory routing settings.

For the migratory model, a novel integer-programming formulation yields a schedule with makespan (e / (e−1))·OPT + P, where P is the time the slowest peer needs to download the file. For the non-migratory model — known to be NP-hard — we give a schedule of makespan 18·OPT + P, the first approximation algorithm for the heterogeneous, asymmetric setting.
